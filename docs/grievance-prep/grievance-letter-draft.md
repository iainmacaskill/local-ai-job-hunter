# Formal grievance letter — skeleton

Do **not** send this until John has reviewed it and the timeline/evidence is
complete. A grievance is most effective sent once, complete and accurate —
not amended afterwards. Check the staff handbook for who the grievance should
be addressed to (usually your manager, or their manager / HR if the grievance
concerns your manager).

Keep the tone factual and calm throughout. The strength is in dates and
specifics, not adjectives. Where the draft says [date] / [name] / [details],
pull them from the completed timeline.

---

[Your address]
[Date]

To: [appropriate manager / HR, per the grievance policy]

**Formal grievance**

Dear [Name],

I am writing to raise a formal grievance under the [organisation]'s grievance
procedure. I am currently absent from work on the advice of my GP, and the
matters set out below are the cause of that absence. I am being supported by
my trade union representative, [John — full name], and I ask that he be
copied into correspondence about this grievance.

My grievance concerns the following matters.

**1. Inappropriate and sexualised comments**

On 24 December 2025, [name] said/did [specific words/actions, as close to
verbatim as possible]. [Witness names, if any.]
[Add any earlier or later incidents in the same pattern, each with date,
words used, and witnesses. If incidents continued after December, set them
out — most recent included.]
These comments were unwanted, and I found them humiliating and offensive. I
consider this conduct to be sexual harassment.

**2. Being undermined in my role**

[Specific dated examples: decisions overruled, criticism in front of others,
duties removed, exclusion from meetings/communications — who, when,
witnesses.]

**3. Staffing pressures and excessive workload**

[When staffing fell short, what the impact was, when you raised it and with
whom, what response you received.]

**4. Change to my home-working arrangements**

[What the arrangement was and since when; when and how it was changed; whether
you were consulted; the reason given, if any; the impact on you.]

**5. Christmas working**

[What you were required to work over Christmas, how that compared with what
had been agreed and/or with the treatment of colleagues.]

**6. Fire safety and safeguarding concerns**

[What you identified, when, and to whom you reported it; what action was or
was not taken. Be specific — these are matters of public interest, not only
personal grievance.]

**Effect on my health**

The combined effect of these matters has had a serious impact on my health.
[Brief factual summary: when symptoms began, when you were signed off, what
your fit notes cite.] My GP has advised that I am not currently fit to return
to work.

**Outcome sought**

I would like:

1. these matters to be investigated fully and impartially;
2. the harassment and the conduct described above to be acknowledged and
   addressed;
3. the fire safety and safeguarding concerns to be acted upon; and
4. a discussion about how my employment can continue — or conclude — in a
   way that protects my health and treats me with dignity.

I am willing to attend a grievance meeting, accompanied by my union
representative, when my health allows, and I ask that any meeting be
arranged with reasonable adjustments given my current ill health.

Yours sincerely,

[Name]

---

## Notes (not part of the letter)

- **Outcome 4 is deliberate.** It opens the door to an exit conversation
  without asking for one outright, and without weakening the grievance. John
  may prefer different wording — his call.
- **Don't overstate.** Anything the employer can factually rebut weakens the
  whole letter. If you're unsure of a date, say "on or around".
- **The safeguarding/fire items** are qualitatively different from the
  personal treatment items — they show you acting responsibly in the
  employer's and public's interest, which reads well and may carry
  whistleblowing protection. Keep them factual.
- The grievance does **not** pause tribunal time limits (John's point). You
  aren't planning to claim, but keep it in mind if anything changes — the
  clock runs regardless of any internal process.
