# Incident timeline (for John)

John asked for: *"a short timeline setting out what happened, when it
happened, who was involved and whether anyone witnessed it."*

Work through this in date order. Precision matters more than polish — exact
dates where you have them, "week commencing…" or "around mid-November" where
you don't. If an entry has supporting evidence (email, message, note, photo),
reference it and add it to the evidence folder.

## How to fill each entry

| Field | What to record |
|---|---|
| **Date** | Exact if possible; otherwise best estimate and say so |
| **What happened** | Facts only — what was said/done, quote words where you remember them |
| **Who** | Who did/said it, and who else was involved |
| **Witnesses** | Anyone who saw or heard it (or was told about it at the time) |
| **Reported?** | Did you tell anyone — manager, HR, colleague? When, how, and what response? |
| **Evidence** | Emails, messages, rota copies, notes made at the time |

## Themes to cover (from the case so far)

Make sure the timeline captures at least one dated entry for each of these,
plus anything else that belongs:

1. **Inappropriate / sexualised comments** — including the incident on
   **24 December 2025**, and *any incident after that date*, however minor it
   felt. This matters enormously: tribunal time limits run from the **last**
   act in a linked series, so more recent incidents change the legal position
   — and therefore your negotiating position — even though you don't intend
   to claim. Include comments, "jokes", messages, anything.
2. **Being undermined** — decisions overruled, criticism in front of others,
   responsibilities removed, being excluded.
3. **Staffing pressures and workload** — dates when you raised concerns about
   staffing levels or workload, and what response you got.
4. **Change to home-working arrangements** — when the arrangement was in
   place, when and how it was changed, whether you were consulted, what
   reason was given.
5. **Christmas working** — what you were required to do, how it compared to
   others / to what was agreed.
6. **Fire safety and safeguarding concerns** — what you identified, when and
   to whom you reported it, and what (if anything) was done.
   *Note for John: reporting genuine safety/safeguarding concerns may count
   as protected disclosures (whistleblowing) — worth flagging to him
   explicitly, as it can strengthen the overall position.*
7. **Health impact** — when symptoms started, GP visits, when you were first
   signed off, what the fit notes say (e.g. work-related stress), any
   medication or referrals.
