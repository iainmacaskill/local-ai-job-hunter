# Evidence checklist

Everything John asked for, plus items that strengthen an exit negotiation.
Tick off as gathered. Keep copies **outside work systems** (personal email,
home printouts, personal cloud folder) — if access to work email is cut off
during any process, you must not lose your evidence.

> If you need to forward work emails to a personal address, forward only what
> relates to your own case, and mention to John that you have done so — some
> employers raise data-protection objections, and he can advise on the safest
> way to preserve evidence (e.g. a data subject access request instead).

## 1. Sickness absence (John's list)

- [ ] All fit notes / sickness certificates, in date order
- [ ] Invitations to any sickness absence / review meetings
- [ ] Notes or minutes from those meetings (ask HR for their copies if you
      don't have them)
- [ ] Any Occupational Health referral letters or reports
- [ ] Correspondence about sick pay (payslips showing SSP / company sick pay)

## 2. Workplace incidents (John's list)

- [ ] Emails, texts, WhatsApp/Teams messages relating to the inappropriate
      comments — including anything sent around **24 December 2025**
- [ ] Any notes you made at the time (diary entries, notes app — export with
      dates visible)
- [ ] Messages showing you reported or mentioned incidents to anyone
- [ ] Anything showing the undermining behaviour (emails overruling you,
      messages excluding you, etc.)
- [ ] Rotas / emails about Christmas working
- [ ] Correspondence about the home-working change (what was agreed before,
      the instruction changing it, any reason given)
- [ ] Emails or reports raising staffing/workload concerns, and replies
- [ ] Anything documenting the fire safety and safeguarding concerns you
      raised — reports, emails, incident logs — and the employer's response

## 3. Contractual / policy documents (useful for the exit negotiation)

- [ ] Employment contract (notice period, sick pay terms, any PILON clause)
- [ ] Staff handbook — especially the **grievance**, **sickness absence**,
      **dignity at work / anti-harassment**, and **home working** policies
- [ ] Last 3–6 payslips (needed to value any settlement properly)
- [ ] Pension scheme details
- [ ] Record of accrued but untaken holiday
- [ ] Length of service confirmation (start date)

## 4. Health impact

- [ ] Summary of GP involvement (dates of appointments, what the fit notes
      cite — e.g. "work-related stress")
- [ ] Any referrals, prescriptions, or counselling connected to the situation

## 5. The timeline

- [ ] Completed `timeline.md` — this is the single most useful document for
      John and should go with the first batch of evidence
